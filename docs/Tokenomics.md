Abstract
The legacy Web3 ecosystem relies on "ponzinomics"—inflationary token printing designed to temporarily subsidize node operators. WaveCollapse abandons this model to build a compliance-native infrastructure designed specifically for settling Real-World Assets (RWAs).
The network is secured and operated via Real Yield Tokenomics. This architecture transforms node operators from crypto-speculators into traditional infrastructure providers earning predictable, cash-flowing dividends.

2. Node Architecture & Staking Mechanics
The network relies on three distinct node types, each serving a unique operational and regulatory function.
A. Validator Nodes (Security)
Validators provide the core cryptographic consensus and block creation that secures the ledger.
Staking Asset: Strictly native Layer 1 $WAVE tokens to maintain sovereign network security.
Minimum Stake: A Fiat-Pegged Dynamic Minimum, equivalent to $100,000 USD dynamically adjusted via decentralized oracle. This mechanism ensures the staking minimum remains stable in fiat value, protecting operators from speculative price swings (see Section 8).
The Yield: Validators earn the lion's share (8–9 bps) of the global macro network volume, distributed proportionally based on the weight of their staked $WAVE.
B. Facilitator Nodes (Liquidity / GAaaS)
Operated by B2B Treasury Managers and Fintech infrastructure providers, Facilitators act as automated relayers providing Gas Abstraction as a Service (GAaaS). They allow Client Applications to subsidize network gas, ensuring retail end-users never experience Web3 friction.
Staking Asset: A Dual-Asset "SLA Bond." Operators stake a base in $WAVE but can add liquid stablecoins (USDC/USDT) to increase their routing bandwidth.
Minimum Stake (Tiered):
Tier 1 (Regional Relay): 10,000 $WAVE minimum. Unlocks capped daily routing volume.
Tier 2 (Global Infrastructure): 100,000 $WAVE minimum. Unlocks unlimited routing bandwidth.
Principal Protection: When a Facilitator fronts the gas to settle a transaction, 100% of that gas cost is atomically reimbursed in the same block from the Client Application's on-chain Paymaster contract, eliminating counterparty risk.
The Yield: Facilitators earn a pure micro-spread (1–3 bps) on the specific, local transaction volume their node successfully routes and sponsors.
C. Archive Nodes (Compliance)
Archive nodes exist strictly to satisfy SEC Rule 17a-4 Software-Defined WORM compliance. They are passive, read-only entities utilized by institutional risk officers to pipe network state into secure cloud storage (e.g., AWS S3).
Staking Requirement: None.
The Yield: 0 bps. They do not participate in consensus.

3. Consensus Innovation: Dynamic Threshold Committees (DTC)
A critical vulnerability in standard Proof-of-Stake (PoS) models occurs when a small validator processes a massive RWA trade (e.g., a $50M commercial property settlement). If the profit from acting maliciously outweighs the validator's staked collateral, the network is fundamentally insecure.
WaveCollapse solves this via Dynamic Threshold Committees. Instead of relying on static validators, the protocol dynamically groups randomized validators together until their aggregate stake exceeds the fiat value of the block they are validating (inclusive of a 150% safety margin).
By assembling these dynamic squads on demand, the protocol mathematically guarantees that the Cost of Corruption always exceeds the Profit from Corruption, successfully securing massive institutional volume without sacrificing decentralization.

5. The Micro-Transaction Shield (Edge Batching Architecture)
While a 15 bps fee model generates massive revenue on institutional settlements, it breaks on retail micro-transactions where fixed gas costs outpace percentage-based yields. To protect Facilitator Node profitability, WaveCollapse provides an architectural framework for Edge Batching.
Client Abstraction: Retail applications building on WaveCollapse do not settle every micro-action (e.g., earning a $0.05 cashback reward) on Layer 1. Instead, they accumulate closed-loop, off-chain utility points within their own ecosystems.
The Settlement Trigger: The Layer 1 is only engaged when a user's off-chain balance crosses a set fiat threshold (e.g., $5,000 cumulative value).
Batch Conversion: Once triggered, the Client Application batches the transaction, calling the Facilitator Node to convert the accumulated off-chain value into a compliant WAVE-S fractional equity asset. This ensures node operators remain highly profitable regardless of the end-user's initial transaction size.

8. Network Protection & Speculator Defense
Because $WAVE is a fixed-supply asset generating institutional cash flow, it is highly susceptible to retail speculator "liquidity squeezes" on public exchanges. WaveCollapse utilizes two core defensive mechanics to ensure infrastructure providers are never priced out of operating nodes:
Fiat-Pegged Dynamic Minimums: Staking minimums are pegged to fiat value via a decentralized oracle. If market speculators drive the price of $WAVE from $1.00 to $10.00, the smart contract automatically adjusts a Tier 2 Facilitator requirement from 100,000 tokens down to 10,000 tokens, keeping the fiat barrier to entry perfectly stable.
The Institutional OTC Desk: Massive funds requiring significant capital to spin up nodes do not source $WAVE from the open DEX (which causes price slippage). Instead, they sign a Service Level Agreement (SLA) with the Foundation to purchase $WAVE over-the-counter. These tokens are cryptographically time-locked exclusively for node staking.

9. Cryptoeconomic Penalties & Tiered Slashing Mechanics
To maintain absolute network integrity, both Validator and Facilitator nodes are bound by automated cryptoeconomic penalties. Because enterprise-grade node operations differ vastly from retail staking, WaveCollapse separates slashing conditions by node type and intent, ensuring mathematical security without punishing accidental hardware faults.
A. Validator Nodes (T2/T3 Consensus Penalties) Validator nodes operating within the T2/T3 Consensus framework and Dynamic Threshold Committees (DTC) face tiered penalties based on the severity of the infraction.
Soft Slashing (Liveness/Latency Faults): In enterprise environments, servers occasionally crash or experience network latency. If a validator drops offline or fails to participate in their assigned DTC, they are penalized with a minor 0.1% to 1% Soft Slash of their staked $WAVE. They are temporarily "jailed" (removed from the active mempool) until the operator restores the node to meet Service Level Agreements (SLAs).
Tier 2 Hard Slashing (Safety Faults & Misconfigurations): The most common "attack" in enterprise node operation is accidental double-signing caused by backup servers spinning up simultaneously with a primary server. To avoid an institutional chilling effect, these provably accidental safety faults incur a 5% to 15% Tier 2 Hard Slash, rather than total principal annihilation.
Tier 3 Catastrophic Slashing (Malicious Action): If a validator committee is caught coordinating a malicious attack—such as intentionally altering transaction data, censoring a specific WAVE-S asset settlement, or attempting an exploit against the DTC—the protocol triggers a Tier 3 Catastrophic Slash. The entire principal stake of the malicious committee members is instantly revoked. Because the DTC ensures the committee's aggregate stake is always 150% of the block's fiat value, this mechanism mathematically guarantees the slashed amount will always exceed the value the bad actors attempted to steal.
B. Facilitator Nodes (Liquidity & Routing Penalties) Because Facilitator Nodes do not participate in block creation, they cannot be slashed for consensus faults. Instead, their penalties are tied strictly to their SLAs for Gas Abstraction as a Service (GAaaS).
SLA Violations (Griefing & Routing Failures): If a Facilitator accepts a routing request from a Client Application (like BrikFi) but fails to execute the gas subsidy—effectively freezing the retail end-user's transaction—they incur an SLA Violation.
Downgrade & Penalty: Repeated griefing or routing failures result in a partial slashing of the operator's $WAVE SLA Bond. Additionally, the node is automatically downgraded from Tier 2 (Global Infrastructure) to Tier 1 (Regional Relay) routing bandwidth, severely limiting their capacity to earn the 1–3 bps settlement spread.
C. The Deflationary Burn Mechanism To continuously increase the value of the network's sovereign security layer, 100% of all slashed $WAVE tokens are permanently burned. They are never recycled into the Protocol Treasury. This deflationary mechanic automatically rewards good actors by making their staked $WAVE mathematically scarcer every time a negligent or malicious operator violates network integrity.
